We are otonomic.

In 2012, Omri Allouche tried to create a website for his cousin, a pastry chef. He thought that as a founder of a software company that served clients like P&G and Greenpeace, it will be easy. It wasn't.

Omri founded Otonomic to change the way small businesses do marketing online. His PhD thesis about ecological communities taught him the importance of diversity for the sustainability of a community. Small businesses change the way our communities are run, and make our economy stronger. To do that, a small businesse needs to be let to do business.

Together with Edik Mitelman and Ilan Lichtnaier, who took part in introducing the community toolbar at Conduit and improving the web experience for millions of users and a team of devoted professionals, Otonomic was created to make sure you don't work for your website – it's your website that works for you.

All of us here at Otonomic know what it’s like to be a small business trying to grow and devote yourself to what you love to do. It’s why we decided to create a product to empower you with the right website.

Whether you are a freelancer, a shop owner, or a professional in your field, you can now compete with the big boys without having to spend tons of money or time on programmers or graphic designers.

Our goal was to make the process of having your own site as effortless as possible, to give you the ability to manage it yourself and not rely on anyone else.
